Heart Disease: What You Should Know

Warning Signs

  • • Chest pain, tightness, or pressure
  • • Shortness of breath at rest or with activity
  • • Pain radiating to arm, neck, jaw, or back
  • • Irregular or rapid heartbeat (palpitations)
  • • Unusual fatigue or weakness
  • • Dizziness or light-headedness
  • • Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet

Key Risk Factors

Modifiable

  • • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • • High cholesterol (LDL)
  • • Smoking
  • • Obesity / overweight
  • • Physical inactivity
  • • Diabetes or pre-diabetes
  • • Excessive alcohol

Non-Modifiable

  • • Age (risk rises after 45/55)
  • • Sex (men at higher early risk)
  • • Family history of heart disease

Prevention Tips

  • • Exercise at least 150 min/week (moderate intensity)
  • • Eat a heart-healthy diet: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein
  • • Quit smoking and limit alcohol
  • • Maintain a healthy weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)
  • • Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol regularly
  • • Manage stress through mindfulness or relaxation
  • • Get 7-9 hours of sleep per night
  • • Take prescribed medications consistently

Understanding Your Metrics

Blood Pressure

Normal: < 120/80 mmHg

Concern: ≥ 130/80 mmHg

Elevated BP is the leading modifiable risk factor.

Total Cholesterol

Normal: < 200 mg/dL

Concern: ≥ 240 mg/dL

High LDL cholesterol promotes arterial plaque.

Fasting Blood Sugar

Normal: < 100 mg/dL

Concern: ≥ 126 mg/dL

Diabetes doubles the risk of heart disease.

Resting Heart Rate

Normal: 60-100 bpm

Concern: > 100 bpm (rest)

Lower resting HR generally indicates better fitness.

About the Dataset

This tool's risk model is derived from a combined heart disease dataset of approximately 7,000 records sourced from the Cleveland, Hungarian, Switzerland, Long Beach VA, and Statlog datasets. The model uses a simplified logistic-regression-inspired scoring system across 11 clinical features. While educational, it captures key patterns observed in clinical data — including the importance of exercise-induced angina, ST segment changes, chest pain type, and traditional risk factors like age, blood pressure, and cholesterol.

For educational purposes only. This tool does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for medical concerns.
